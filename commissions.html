Commissions & Art works

For Wasel, there’s nothing like the thrill of creating art for an emerging structure or an existing space. Even with more than ten years as a professional artist and major commissions under his belt from international companies such as Nissan and CapitaLand, each project brings a new exploration into art and design.

Wasel and his project management team study each site, space, and architectural elements and provide a detailed quote for each project based on the stated goals of the project. Contact us if you have a project in mind. We’ll be delighted to provide an initial consultation or to request references from the architects and designers we’ve teamed up with to create the works you see here.

References happily provided upon request.
If you’re in the Bay Area, please visit Ærena Gallery at Ma(i)sonry Napa Valley, California to view his recent works.

The 66 Days Project

Client: CapitaLand | Launched 2012 | Medium: A series of 5 paintings, 212.59 x 94.48 in, Oil on wood

Project Summary

When one of Asia's largest real estate development companies was in the process of building five luxury residential towers, they commissioned Wasel to create an art work for each tower. The kicker? All five large-scale paintings had to be completed in 66 days.

CapitaLand Limited oversaw the building and design of the five residential towers that comprise Rihan Heights in Abu Dhabi, capital city of United Arab Emirates. The client’s stated goal in commissioning Wasel was to elevate the modern elegance of the luxurious development to an entirely new level.


Wasel was given the mission of creating a unique design identity for each building based on its color scheme while maintaining unity with the overall vision of Will Alsop, the project’s architect. The client, working with the designer, also gave Wasel parameters for working with the existing art and design. Each of Wasel’s paintings needed to provide a bold focus for the lobby while harmoniously assimilating with the existing art lighting installation.

The entire project was completed on time and ahead of schedule to meet the the grand opening gala for the towers. From inception and initial client meetings to final installation of the five works, the project was completed in 66 days.

World Of Music And Dance

Client: WOMAD.org | Launched 2010 | Medium: 314.4 x 93.6 in, Oil on wood

Project SUMMARY

Founded in 1980 by Peter Gabriel, the WOMAD (World of Music, Arts and Dance) Festival presented Wasel with an opportunity he couldn’t resist: Paint an entire canvas in three days while crowds watched him work. The performance painting took place over three days in Abu Dhabi under a blazing sun with music from the nearby stage surrounding Wasel as he worked. Standing nearly eight feet high, the resulting painting stretched for more than 26 feet of sand and sky in the desert of Abu Dhabi.


The first WOMAD festival was held in Shepton Mallet, United Kingdom in 1982 by Gabriel and fellow organizers Jonathan Arthur, Thomas Brooman, Martin Elbourne, Bob Hooton, Mark Kidel, and Stephen Pritchard. In 2014, the festival continues to be a renowned international event celebrating multi-cultural art from around the world.

ZAFCO

Client: ZAFCO / AK Design | Launched 2007 | 14 Paintings commissioned

Project Summary

Each project has a distinct goal, which offers a unique set of problems to solve. For Zafco’s sleek Dubai headquarters, the challenge was to create a series of vibrant works that would reflect Zafco’s status as one of the Middle East’s largest importers. That in itself wouldn’t have been exactly unusual, but there was a caveat: The CEO wanted the series of paintings to be uniform in appearance, he wanted nothing less than perfect unity.

Wasel’s solution? Paint them at one time. His idea was to create one (very) long painting. The entire work stretched more than 43 feet long, as can be seen in the photo. After completion, the single work was divided it into 6 separate pieces for Zafco’s offices and meeting rooms. Eight additional works were created for the lobby and other public spaces throughout the building.


Angela Blair, Sr. Interior Designer at AK Design Architecture & Interiors commissioned the work on behalf of Zafco.

Formula 1: The Art of Racing

Client: KHP Consulting | Launched 2009 | Medium: Acrylic on Replica

Project Summary

Formula 1 racing is one of the United Arab Emirates’ biggest sporting events. To celebrate the sport, they chose one artist to do the unthinkable: Paint over a Ferrari. For a series entitled “The Art of Racing,” Wasel was commissioned to paint a full-size replica of a Ferrari Formula 1 car.

Another live painting performance, Wasel blanketed the Ferrari model with his unmistakable lines and wild beast palette of colors while curious onlookers watched him create the first Formula 1 art car in the history of racing.


Under the direction of KHP Consulting, The Art of Racing event was designed to bring together the worlds of motorsport and fine art by showcasing the country’s artistic talent. Wasel’s Ferrari Formula 1 car was popular with crowds and exhibited throughout Abu Dhabi, UAE.

Art Cars Exhibition, Saadiyat Island

Client: Abu Dhabi Tourism Authority | Launched 2009 | Chevrolet Impala 1959

Project Summary

Saadiyat Island is known as the future home of a Guggenheim Museum and Le Louvre Abu Dhabi, but the Island’s first-ever art event was the Art Cars Exhibition. Held in honor of the opening of the Abu Dhabi Grand Prix, the Art Cars Exhibition presented an eclectic mix of classic cars from Europe and America customized by acclaimed international artists. Wasel’s vehicle (and canvas) was a 1959 Chevy Impala.

At the event, Jeff Koons asked for an artist-to-artist viewing of the work with Wasel. Friends since 2007, Wasel first met Koons at the opening day reception for Art Paris and they met on numerous occasions in the Middle East.

Nissan Murano

Client: Nissan | Launched 2006 |Medium: Sculpture in wood

Project Summary

When one of the world’s largest car companies wanted to more closely align their brand with artistic endeavor, they came to Wasel. For Nissan’s “Sculpture in Motion” event, Wasel was commissioned to create a piece of sculpture that captured the Nissan's Murano Model tagline: “Sculpture in Motion.”


Given the theme of “reinterpreting the Murano through the eyes of the artist,” Wasel created a boldly colored and shaped work of three-dimensional art that moves through space with jagged forms and razor sharp lines that seem to be bursting in every direction at once.
