The 66 Days Project

Client: CapitaLand | Launched 2012 | Medium: 15.7ft x 7.8ft Oil on wood

Project Summary

CapitaLand Limited is a Singapore based real estate company. Formed November 2000, as a result of a merger between DBS Land and Pidemco Land, it is one of Asia's largest real estate companies. CapitaLand oversaw the building and design of five residential towers, Rihan Heights, in Abu Dhabi, the Capital of United Arab Emirates.

The client’s goal was to enhance the lobby space of the newly built Rihan Heights, luxury residential towers with original artworks. Artist Wasel Choi was commissioned to create 5 original oil paintings for each of the 5 towers. The aim was to give a unique personality to each tower and elevate the modern elegance of Rihan Heights to a new level. The Client implicitly requested that each painting be the centerpiece of the lobby while harmoniously assimilating with the architectural structure and ceiling light installation.

WASELROSA managed the project from its inception; including the project proposal, onsite client meetings, budgeting and bringing the project to its completion on time to meet the opening schedule of the residential towers.

World Of Music And Dance

Client: WOMAD.org | Launched 2010 | Medium: 26.2ft x 7.8ft Oil on wood

Project SUMMARY

WOMAD (World of Music, Arts and Dance) is an internationally established Festival bringing together artist from all over the globe. WOMAD was founded in 1980 by Peter Gabriel, Thomas Brooman and Bob Hooton. The first WOMAD festival was held in Shepton Mallet, UK in 1982 and continues to excite, inform, and create awareness of the worth and potential of a multicultural society.

WOMAD commissioned Wasel to do live painting performance and produce a large-scale painting on site during the 3 days of WOMAD festival as a celebration of Arts and Music held in Abu Dhabi.

ZAFCO

Client: ZAFCO / AK Design | Launched 2007 | 14 Paintings commissioned

Project Summary

Angela Blair, Sr. Interior Designer at AK Design Architecture & Interiors came to Wasel’s solo exhibition, Constructivism & UAEism. She was impressed by Wasel’s work and later commissioned him to create 14 paintings for Zafco. The challenge of this project was to complement the corporate image of Zafco’s state of art UAE Headquarters, reflecting the brand, the culture, and the energy of this vibrant, rapidly expanding company that is the leading imoprter and exporter of high quality automotive tires. Wasel’s use of dynamic line and color was well suited for the simple black and white palette of the company. To me the company CEO’s request of creating a series of paintings that are similar and unvarying, Wasel came up with a very unconventional solution. He painted a 43.31ft x 2.95ft long painting and divided it into 6 separate pieces for Zafco’s offices and meeting rooms.

Formula 1: The Art of Racing

Art Cars Exhibition, Saadiyat Island

Client: Abu Dhabi Tourism Authority | Launched 2009 | Chevrolet Impala 1959

Project Summary

On 25th October, the Art Cars exhibition was held in the grounds of Manarat Al Saadiyat until November 30th, 2009 - an intriguing mix of classic cars from Europe and America that have been customized by local and international artists.

Programed to coincide with the start of the inaugural Abu Dhabi Grand Prix, 13 cars were transformed into functional art objects inspired by Pop Art, the visual art movement made famous by artists like Andy Warhol in the 1950s and 60s. Decorated cars include a 1931 Chevrolet Independence, a Mercedes 250SL 1968, a Cadillac LaSalle 1939 and even a Ford T 1920, one of the first cars to be manufactured, back in the roaring 20s of the US.

Wasel was one of the first artist to be selected to participate in the Art Cars exhibition.

Burj Khalifa

Nissan Murano

Client: TBWA\RAAD | Launched 2006 | Sculpture in wood 7'87" x 6'56" x 3'28"

Project Summary

The event “Sculpture in Motion” aimed at raising cultural awareness, and as an opportunity for the company to brand itself with an artistic endeavor which will be mutually beneficial for the client, the company and the artist. The event was positioned as the first of an annual initiative by the company to promote their products alongside the arts.

Wasel was commissioned to create an art sculpture/installation involving the Nissan company tag line “Sculpture in Motion.” The artist is to produce the work on site around the theme ‘the reinterpretation of the Murano through the eyes of the artist.’
