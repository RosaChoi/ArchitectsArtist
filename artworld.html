Art World

Born in United Arab Emirates. Living in San Francisco. A world of vibrant experiences in between. Wasel’s love of travel and cultures across the globe has been an abiding source of inspiration behind his work. After his first artist program abroad in Czech Republic, Wasel accepted artist residencies in the Germany and Kenya. He has also worked extensively in Singapore, Malaysia, and China, most often in large-scale oil on canvas. His works are in the permanent collections of corporations, museums, hotels, schools, government organizations, residential developments, royal families, and dignitaries.

Today, Wasel paints from his new home in San Francisco and finds limitless inspiration exploring the natural beauty of Northern California with his wife, Rosa.

If you’re in the San Francisco Bay Area, contact Wasel to view works from the current collection.

USA | A Newfound Home

Current works: May 2013 - Present

Wasel didn’t just move to San Francisco, California in 2013 with his wife; he found a new home. Inspired by his surroundings and the vast beauty of the West, he can’t imagine wanting to live anywhere else. Since moving to Northern California, Wasel's drawings have taken on a new refinement and his works show the inspiration of the natural beauty that surrounds him. You might find him sketching in San Francisco, scoping a corporate commission in Silicon Valley or stretching a spectacularly oversized canvas in Marin County.

Singapore | Happily Asian

Lines: July 2012 - May 2013

Singapore has a very special place in Wasel’s heart, and on his marriage certificate. Wasel and his American wife, Rosa, were married in Singapore. During his time in Singapore, Wasel continued his relationship with Steve Qwek, an architect at CapitaLand, whom he worked closely together with on the Rihan Heights project in Abu Dhabi. During this time, he met with the President of Singapore and continued his commission work, including artwork for the current Ambassador to Switzerland. He even picked up some Singlish.

UAE | The Multicultural Beginning

Artwork: January 2007 - June 2012

International by even the most cosmopolitan standards, the United Arab Emirates is home to a community of more than 240 nationalities. Wasel was raised in the city of Al Ain amidst the vastness of Rubʿ al Khal, known in English as “The Empty Quarter.” The world's largest sand desert, The Empty Quarter covers most of the lower third of the Arabian Peninsula. Here, amidst vast stretches of sand and sky, Wasel developed his thirst for large-scale works.

Germany | Beauty Discovered

Lines: December 2011 - January 2012

Seeing snow for the first time as an adult was a revelation; The first of many discoveries Wasel would make during his artist residency in Germany. Invited to Germany by the State Minister of Saxony, Dr. Johanes Beermann, Wasel was based in Dresden for the period of his artist residency. During that time he lived with local artists Jan Karusse, Thomas Reichstein and Tobias Stengel, and was warmly welcomed into the German art world by prominent artists such as Stefan Plenkers.

Wasel's first visit to China was to participate in the International Exhibit at the Expo 2010 Shanghai. Shortly after arriving in China, Wasel knew he wanted to stay. Wasel traveled from Shanghai to Beijing to all the way to Changchun. What started as a business trip to China turned into a visual and cultural journey.

Kenya | Living Simplicity

Lines: March 2010

At the invitation of UNICEF Ambassador Dr. Tegla Chepkite Loroupe, Wasel became the Artist in Residence at the Tegla Loroupe Peace Foundation in Kenya. In addition to her work with UNICEF, Dr. Loroupe is the United Nations African Athlete Ambassador and an Oxfam Ambassador in Darfur. During his stay in Kenya, Wasel lived with the Pokot tribe. Living simply among their tribe, he felt gratitude and humility in a way never before experienced. His works during this time are almost exclusively ink drawings on paper.

Lines | Kenya 12″ X 17″ Ink on Paper

Czech Republic | Friendship Forged

Artwork: April 2009

Wasel’s first artistic stint was in the Czech Republic when he was one of 12 artists selected to participate in SMALT ART, an international Art Exhibition. Held at VÍTKOVICE Holding Co., a 180-year-old factory in Ostrava, Czech Republic, the factory closed down operations for seven days for the artists to paint in enamel on steel panels. The painted panels then went through the factory's furnaces for 10 minutes at 1562 F°/850 C°.
Wasel created six steel panels measuring 58.27” x 106.3” (148cm x 270cm), which were exhibited to the public. Wasel cherishes the friendships he made during this time with the Czech Republic's prominent artists Aleš Lamr and Václav Malina.
